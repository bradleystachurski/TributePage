Timeline
  • December 10, 2012 University of Colorado announces that it was hiring MacIntyre to replace Jon Embree.
  • September 2, 2013 MacIntyre wins his opening game over rival Colorado State. This is his only victory of the 2012 season.
  • End of 2012 Season Finishes first season of coaching Colorado at 1-11. There's work to be done.
  • Endof of 2013 Season Finishes second season at 4-8. Improvement, but still a lot of work to be done.
  • February 20, 2014 University of Colorado Board of Regents extends MacIntyre's contract through 2018.
  • End of 2014 Season Finishes season 2-10.
  • End of 2015 Season Finishes season 4-9.
  • September 2, 2016 First game of 2016 season. Colorado posts an impressive victory vs instate rival, Colorado State winning 44 - 7. The beginning of the Rise.
  • September 10, 2016 Colorado soundly defeats Idaho State 56 - 7.
  • September 17, 2016 Colorado loses to Michigan in Ann Arbor 28 - 45, however Colorado had a strong start to the game and was beating Michigan 21 - 7 at the end of the first quarter. Senior quarterback Sefo Liufau goes out with an injury which turned the momentum of the game. Colorado begins to show they are a serious opponent to any team in the nation.
  • September 24, 2016 Colorado has its first Pac-12 victory vs Oregon, winning with an interception to seal a victory as the last play of the game. Colorado wins 41 - 38. The Rise is real.
  • October 1, 2016 Colorado makes a statement after beating Oregon the prior week. They defeat Oregon State 47 - 6, establishing themselves as one of the best teams in the Pac-12.
  • October 8, 2016 Colorado loses its second game of the season to USC 17 - 21.
  • October 15, 2016 Colorado responds to prior loss with an impressive victory vs Arizona State 16 - 40.
  • October 22, 2016 Colorado posts its first ever victory vs Standford, beating them 10 - 5 on the road.
  • November 3, 2016 Colorado defeats UCLA 20 - 10.
  • November 12, 2016 Colorado crushes Arizona 49 - 24.
  • November 19 2016 Colorado faces 23rd ranked Washington State at home and soundly defeats them 38 - 24, placing them in the position to win vs Utah the following week to put them in the Pac-12 Championship game at Levi Stadium in San Francisco. The Rise is Real.
  • November 24, 2016 (current date of writing) Colorado enjoys Thanksgiving day before facing Utah on Saturday November, 26. This team has the potential of being one of the greatest turnarounds a Division I college football program has ever seen. Welcome to the fight.
